How to Spot Tattoo & Ink Scams in 2026

By scam.ink | February 21, 2026 | 8 min read

Tattoo scams range from dangerous counterfeit inks containing heavy metals to fake artists stealing deposits. In 2026, social media has made it easier than ever for scammers to present stolen portfolio work as their own.

Common Tattoo Scams

  • Counterfeit ink products — containing lead, mercury, and bacteria. Sold on Amazon and AliExpress
  • Stolen portfolio artists — using other artists' work to book appointments
  • Deposit theft — collecting deposits then disappearing
  • Unsafe home studios — no sterilization, no licensing, no accountability
  • Fake tattoo removal — unregulated laser services causing scarring

How to Protect Yourself

  • Verify artist portfolios with reverse image search
  • Check state licensing and health department records
  • Never pay full price upfront — deposits only
  • Research ink brands — look for EU-compliant products
  • Use a VPN when browsing tattoo booking sites
