Stolen Portfolio Artists

Fake Artist

Tattoo 'artists' using stolen photos of other artists' work as their portfolio. Actual skill level is far below what's shown. Result: botched tattoos requiring expensive cover-ups or removal.

Toxic Tattoo Ink Brands

Dangerous Ink

Unregulated ink brands containing heavy metals, carcinogens, and bacteria. EU banned several brands. Amazon and AliExpress still sell them. Causes infections and allergic reactions.

High Risk Active

Deposit Scam Studios

Booking Fraud

Pop-up tattoo studios or Instagram artists collecting $200-500 deposits then closing shop or blocking clients. No refund, no tattoo, no recourse.

High Risk Active

Kitchen Scratchers on Social Media

Unlicensed

Unlicensed home tattooists advertising on Instagram and TikTok with cheap rates. No sterilization, reused needles, no bloodborne pathogen training. High risk of hepatitis and staph.

Medium Risk Active

Fake Tattoo Removal Creams

Fake Product

Creams claiming to remove tattoos painlessly at home for $30-50. Dermatologists confirm these do not work. Some cause chemical burns and scarring.

Medium Risk Active

AI Tattoo Design Theft

Copyright Theft

Services scraping custom tattoo designs from artists and selling them as 'AI-generated originals.' Artists lose commission and creative rights.

Medium Risk Active

Convention Bait-and-Switch

Fake Artist

Tattoo convention booths showing one artist's work but having a different (less skilled) artist do the actual tattooing. Discovered only after the tattoo is started.

Fake Apprenticeship Programs

Course Scam

Online 'tattoo apprenticeship' programs charging $3,000-5,000. Real apprenticeships are in-person at licensed shops. These provide worthless certificates and outdated videos.

UV-reactive inks marketed as 'safe' with no long-term studies. Some contain phosphorus compounds linked to skin cancer. Not FDA-approved for injection.
